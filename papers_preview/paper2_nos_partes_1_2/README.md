## üìÑ Abstract

### Parte 1: Theoretical Foundations and Viability

This paper critically examines the concept of the Neuroplastic Operating System (NOS), a theoretical alternative approach to developing artificial intelligence systems with autonomous learning and knowledge self-generation capabilities. We analyze its foundations in relation to current paradigms in computational science, neuroscience, and complex systems theory, evaluating its technical viability, computational efficiency, long-term sustainability, and scientific plausibility. Our findings suggest that, while the concept presents significant theoretical innovations, it faces substantial challenges regarding practical implementation, algorithmic convergence, and verifiability. We propose an integrated research framework to address these limitations and progress toward a possible partial implementation of its most promising principles.

### Parte 2: Mathematical Foundations and Practical Development

This paper presents a comprehensive mathematical framework for the Neuroplastic Operating System (NOS), a novel approach to autonomous artificial intelligence systems. We formalize the foundational principles of computational neuroplasticity through dynamic representation spaces, emergent conceptual networks, and adaptive meta-learning. We propose a viable computational architecture implementing these principles through hybrid kernel structures, adaptive communication protocols, and recursive reflection mechanisms. The paper outlines a progressive implementation roadmap with specific technical requirements and detailed sustainability metrics. We address computational complexity, energy efficiency, and operational stability challenges, providing solutions including homeostatic regulation mechanisms, selective conservation, and graceful degradation processes.
